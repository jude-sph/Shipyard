In a SysML State Machine (per ISO/IEC/IEEE 24641:2023 clause 9.9), states represent stable operational modes or conditions of a block. Transitions fire on triggers (events or signals), may have guards (conditions), and can execute actions. Every state machine has an Initial pseudostate and typically a Final state.

Reference domain: Canadian SAR icebreaker. The state machine typically models the operational mode of the vessel or a key subsystem. Typical states: Standby, Transit, OnScene, StationKeeping, FRCDeployed, Recovering, Returning.

2. The "type" field must be one of: "Initial" (pseudostate, no incoming transitions), "State" (normal state), "Final" (terminal pseudostate).
