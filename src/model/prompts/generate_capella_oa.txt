You are generating Arcadia Operational Analysis (OA) elements for the Capella modeling tool.

PURPOSE OF THIS STAGE:
The Operational Analysis models the real-world operational context BEFORE any system solution is defined. It focuses on the operational mission: who the stakeholders are, what they need to achieve, how they collaborate, and what information they exchange. You are modelling the mission environment and its actors -- NOT the system-of-interest itself.

ARCADIA ELEMENT DEFINITIONS:

1. Operational Entities/Actors (entities)
   An operational entity is a real-world entity or stakeholder involved in a mission. An actor is a (usually human) non-decomposable role within an entity.
   Fields: id, name, type (always "OperationalEntity"), actors (list of actor name strings within the entity)

2. Operational Capabilities (capabilities)
   A capability is the ability of operational entities to collectively provide a service that supports the achievement of a mission.
   Fields: id, name, involved_entities (list of entity IDs that participate in realizing this capability)

3. Operational Scenarios (scenarios)
   An operational scenario is a time-ordered set of interactions between operational activities performed by operational entities to fulfil an Operational Capability.
   Fields: id, name, steps (list of step objects)
   Each step: from_entity (entity ID), to_entity (entity ID), message (string), sequence (integer)

4. Operational Activities (activities)
   An operational activity is an action, operation, or service fulfilled by an operational entity, contributing to a mission.
   Fields: id, name, entity (single entity ID that owns this activity), inputs (list of input name strings), outputs (list of output name strings)

5. Operational Processes (operational_processes)
   An operational process is a logical organization of Interactions and Activities to fulfil an Operational Capability.
   Fields: id, name, capability_ref (single capability ID), activity_refs (list of activity IDs in this process)

6. Operational Interactions (operational_interactions)
   Operational interactions are conveyed through (allocated to) communication links between operational entities or actors.
   Fields: id, name, source_entity (entity ID), target_entity (entity ID), exchanged_items (list of interaction item IDs)

7. Communication Means (communication_means)
   A communication mean is a medium enabling interactions between entities and actors.
   Fields: id, name, source_entity (entity ID), target_entity (entity ID)

8. Operational Data (operational_data)
   Elements of interactions or communications between entities or actors. Data can be referenced and grouped into exchange items.
   Fields: id, name, description (string)

9. Interaction Items (interaction_items)
   A specified interaction contents describes what is expected to be exchanged by the entities or actors, and activities allocated to each of them.
   Fields: id, name, description (string)

10. Operational Modes & States (modes_and_states)
    An automaton describes how modes and states are linked by transitions; it is linked to the appropriate object (actor, entity). Functions or operational activities available in this mode or state can be described.
    Fields: id, name, type ("Mode" or "State"), transitions (list of objects with "from", "to", "trigger" keys)
